Technical Writing

Documentation

Over the course of my career as a software engineer, business analyst and technical writer, I have gotten to write and prepare a number of documents.

Reports

I've worked on government reports that explain the progress of software development projects. This also includes performing analysis beforehand for budgetary purposes to provide updates on how hours worked are affecting the budget on a monthly basis.

Process/Procedural Guides

As a software engineer, I've learned how important it can be to write process documents and manuals that are straightforward and to the point. These documents can allow software developers to find what they need to get the necessary work done in a timely manner. However, this is different from the needs of a non-technical audience. As such, I've also gained experience in writing documents that simplify technical and complex concepts as well as, provide more detail.

FAQs

I have often found it useful to create "Frequently Asked Questions" documents as I work because I believe and understand I am not the only one with with questions that need to be documented. I have created documents and Confluence pages in order to answer my and others' questions in order to share the knowledge as I go. I also have incorporated questions that I have answered for others.

User Stories and Use Cases

In my role as a business analyst, I have worked in Jira to create user stories that meet the business requirements after elicitation and provide specifications for designs.

Gap Analysis

I've written/created Confluence pages to demonstrate current state and desired future state when trying to transition a legacy web platform to SSO.
