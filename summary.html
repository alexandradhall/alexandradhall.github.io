Summary

Over the course of my career I have worked in a number of roles that deal with software and machinery. When I started worked as a data management technician, I worked with engineers that designed and modeled car parts for the major automobile producers. I learned much about engineering and the principles of data management that are described in the DMBOK. Later on, I moved to a Technical Manual Editor role that worked with the military to make changes to manuals that are hundreds if not thousands of pages long. I learned a great deal about the fundamentals of technical writing which helps me now when writing or editing documents to communicate complex topics to different audiences.

My software career truly began when I became a software engineer. I wanted to challenge myself and work in an environment where I could grow my skills. At this point in time, I was also working on my Bachelor's degree in Software Development, thus I was learning and applying the knowledge at the same time. I was able to learn and make practical use of many object-oriented programming languages as well as others. I specifically worked with a legacy software that worked on aircraft maintenance trainers. Not only did I work on developing the software, but I also grew into the role allowing me to bring in project management skills, business analysis skills and technical writing skills.

I am now working to learn and become a business analyst/technical writer. Both positions have easily transferable skills and build on each other. I'd like to learn and build more business analysis skills so that I may shift more into that role. This website is the portfolio to display the skills I am working to gain while also maintaining my software engineering skills.
